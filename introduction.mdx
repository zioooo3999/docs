<p>
  This document is a Functional Specification Document (FSD) for the development of the Tempe Park website. It serves as the primary reference for defining the functional requirements of the system to be developed, ensuring that all stakeholders including the business, design, and development teams have a unified understanding of the system's features and behavior.
</p>

<p>
  Tempe Park is an Edu-Park and tempeh innovation center that aims to become a hub for education, inspiration, and experiences related to tempeh and its ecosystem. To support this vision, a centralized digital platform is needed, in the form of an official website that can deliver comprehensive information, increase user engagement, and strengthen Tempe Park's image as an educational and innovation destination.
</p>

<p>
  With this document, it is hoped that the development process can run in a structured manner, minimize miscommunication, and ensure that the system being built is in accordance with business objectives and user needs.
</p>

      <Card title="Brand Focus & Positioning" color="#ef4444" icon="file-text" horizontal>
        - Focus on general information (About, Commitment, Program).
        - Tempe Prkhas not yet been highlighted as a "destinationn experience"
        - it feels more like a company profile or institution.
      </Card>

      <Card title="Content Presentation" color="#ef4444" icon="file-text" horizontal tittle="Content Presentation">
        - Lots of text with long descriptions.

        - Underutilizing layout for storytelling.

      <Card title="Call-To-Action (CTA) & Conversion" color="#ef4444" icon="file-text" horizontal>

  <Tab title="2.1.2 Website Baru">
    ### Tampilan Website Baru

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Hero Section (Main Banner Section)" color="#22c55e" icon="palette" horizontal>
        - Using a visually and contextually powerful aerial view of Tempe Park.
        - The headline is clear, strong, and storytelling (“A fun way to learn, play, and taste Indonesia’s heritage”).
        - The CTA is very clear and contrasting (Buy Ticket & Learn More).
        - Provides a first impression that immediately explains value and experience
      </Card>

      <Card title="Content & Information Structure" color="#22c55e" icon="user" horizontal>
        - Struktur berbasis user journey (What's on, Journey, Commitment, Article).
        - Content is grouped, more modularly, and segmented.
        - There is a clear information hierarchy from awarenesss -\> exploration -\> engagement
        - Easier to scan and to understand
      </Card>

      <Card title="Visual Identity(Branding)" color="#22c55e" icon="gauge" horizontal>
        - Using stronger and more consistent green color as an identity
        - More modern visual with use of cards, rounded corners, and shadows
        - The consitency of the design system is more visible (spacing, typography, buttons).
        - The brands feels more premium, natural, and experiential
      </Card>

      <Card title="Experience & Navigation" color="#22c55e" icon="file-text" horizontal>
        - More structured navigation (Plan your visit, Experience, Education, Innovation)
        - Using section-based navigation that support exploration.
        - There are interactive elements such a slider, cards, and highlighted content.
        - Users guided naturally through the experience flow.
      </Card>

      <Card title="Brand Focus & Positioning" color="22c55e" icon="file-text" horizontal>
        - Focus on visitor's experience amd journey.
        - Highlighting Tempe Park as an edu-park & tourist destination
        - there is strong storytelling related to education, activites, and engagement
        - More towards experiential branding (not just information)
      </Card>

      <Card title="Content Presentation" color="#22c55e" icon="file-text" horizontal tittle="Content Presentation">
        - More visual-driven(large images, Card layout).
        - The text is made more concise and impactfull
        - Using grid & card to improve readability.
      </Card>

      <Card title="Call-To-Action (CTA) & Conversion" color="#22c55e" icon="file-text" horizontal>
        - The CTA is clear, consisten and strategic (Buy Ticket, Explore, View All News)
        - Placed at key points in the user journey.
        - More coneversion oriented (tickets, visits, engagement).
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

### 2.1.3 Conclusion

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Differences" color="#6366f1" icon="sparkles">
    The differences between the existing website and Tempe Park's new website design show a significant shift from a conventional informative website to a digital platform that is oriented towards experience and user journey
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Existing" color="#0ea5e9" icon="arrow-trend-up">
    The existing website still focused on conveying general information, with a linear structure, weak visuals, and minimal emphasis on user interaction and conversion. This made the website feel like a standard company profile and failed to represent Tempe Park as a compelling educational tourist destination.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Contrast" color="#10b981" icon="code">
    In contrast, the new website design presents a more modern, structured, and strategic approach. The primary focus is no longer solely on information, but on user experience, brand storytelling, and clear navigation to encourage engagement and conversions, such as ticket purchases or program exploration.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
